Projects

I went out to Philmont Scout Ranch in Cimarron, NM for a 14-day Order of the Arrow Trail Crew trek including service projects, trail-building, backpacking, and wilderness survival.

This is my personal desktop rig that I built. It's running an i7-4790k, 16GB RAM, a GTX 1080, and a 1TB NVMe boot drive. The system has 480mm of total radiator space which keeps everything cool.

I led a 225 man hour eagle scout project at the Medal of Honor Grove, restoring the Nevada & Kentucky acres. You can see more here.

I was one of the Co-Founders of Real Random, LLC. Real Random specializes in Non-Deterministic Random Bit Generation. We have developed a way to capture entropy from unpredictable physical sources.

I worked on a research project to develop wearable devices for Parkinson's patients to help with tremors and freezing of gait. You can watch the NBC story here, or read the UDaily article here.

I designed and built a wearable monitor for harmful environmental conditions. It monitors UV light, VOCs, temperature, humidity, sound level, and magnetic fields.

I won the 2017 UD ECE Autonomous Drone Competition, where we had to program a Raspberry Pi to fly a DJI Matrice 100 drone through a course start to finish with no human intervention. You can read the article here.

I was the president of the Electric Vehicle Club at UD, working on building an electric FSAE car. The car has a 35kW 3-phase AC motor, and a 148V lithium-ion battery pack capable of driving 500A peak current.

This is the crazy mini bike I built. It's a kit frame with a Harbor Freight Predator 212 engine with upgraded carburetor jet, emulsion tube, intake, exhaust, and 8 degrees of timing advance. The top speed is ~35 mph.
